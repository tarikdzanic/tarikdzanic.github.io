TARIK DZANIC

I'm a Ph.D. student in the Department of Ocean Engineering at Texas A&M University and a member of the Computing Scholar Program in the Center for Applied Scientific Computing (CASC) at Lawrence Livermore National Lab.

Research

My research interests are in the development and analysis of high-resolution numerical methods, particularly relating to high-order discontinuous spectral element methods, shock capturing for hyperbolic conservation laws, large-scale computational fluid dynamics, and kinetic schemes for rarefied gas dynamics. I am part of the development team of PyFR (pyfr.org) and a collaborator on the MFEM project (mfem.org).
